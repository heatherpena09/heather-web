We built a robot using LEGO Spike and programmed it to move and perform specific tasks. The robot was coded to control its movements—such as turning and walking a set distance—to successfully navigate through a prepared obstacle course.

We conducted a frog dissection where we carefully opened and examined the specimen. We documented the process through notes and photos, then labeled the internal and external parts for better understanding of its anatomy.
We conducted the gummy bear experiment in our chemistry activity to demonstrate combustion. The gummy bear, when exposed to a strong oxidizing agent, released energy in the form of heat and light, showing the chemical energy stored in sugar.
We conducted a laboratory activity using the microscope to observe different specimens, including onion cells and human cheek cells. Through this, we were able to examine their structures more closely and understand the differences between plant and animal cells..
During our work immersion at the hospital, I was able to observe and experience some tasks in the radiology department. I assisted with basic operations and gained exposure to how radiologic technology is applied in a clinical setting.
